DAYON AETA

Dayon Aeta was founded with the goal of supporting and uplifting the Aeta community, one of the indigenous groups in the Philippines. Inspired by their resilience and rich culture, the organization was created to provide opportunities in education, health, and livelihood.

The name "Dayon" means "Welcome" — symbolizing an open invitation for everyone to extend help, share kindness, and walk alongside the Aeta people toward a brighter future.

THE AETA COMMUNITY

The Aetas are among the earliest indigenous peoples of the Philippines, known for their deep bond with nature and resilience in the mountainous regions of Luzon, particularly in provinces like Zambales, Tarlac, Pampanga, Bataan, and Quezon. For generations, they have lived through farming, hunting, and fishing—using the land's natural resources for food, medicine, and tools. Their rich knowledge of herbal healing and forest ecology remains respected even by non-indigenous communities.

Traditionally nomadic, Aeta groups moved based on resource availability. However, modern challenges such as land development and deforestation have forced many to settle in lowland areas. Despite this, they continue to practice age-old customs like cañao (thanksgiving rituals), tayaw (spiritual dances), and duwaling (traditional songs), keeping their cultural identity alive.

Aeta society is built on equality and cooperation. Decisions are made collectively, guided by elders who are valued for their wisdom rather than wealth. Property is shared rather than individually owned, emphasizing community over personal gain.

Today, more Aetas are engaging in education and livelihood programs while advocating for land rights and cultural recognition. The younger generation strives to balance tradition with modern life.

Their story is one of strength, humility, and unity—a powerful reminder of how to live sustainably and stay connected to one's roots.

CULTURE AND TRADITIONS

Music & Dance – Music is central to Aeta gatherings, serving as entertainment, storytelling, and spiritual expression. They use bamboo instruments like the kulibaw, balingbing, pattanggok, and tongatong, creating sounds inspired by nature. Traditional dances such as tayaw and talipi are performed during rituals and celebrations, often with chants or improvised lyrics. These performances preserve history, strengthen community ties, and honor their ancestors.

 Livelihood – The Aeta lifestyle is rooted in self-sufficiency and harmony with the environment. They cultivate root crops like sweet potato, cassava, and taro through shifting farming methods (kaingin), allowing the land to regenerate naturally. Hunting and gathering remain vital, with men hunting wild animals and women collecting fruits, honey, and medicinal plants. Some Aetas now earn income through handicrafts and selling forest products. Despite modern changes, many continue to practice their traditional ways with pride and resilience.

CHALLENGES

Education – Limited access to schools, teachers, and learning resources.
 Livelihood – Few sustainable job opportunities to support families.
 Healthcare – Difficulty in reaching medical services, especially in remote areas.
 Land Rights – Ongoing challenges in protecting ancestral domains and resources.

WHY  CULTURAL
PRESERVATION  MATTERS

The Aeta's culture is not only their identity but also a vital part of the Philippines' heritage. Preserving their traditions ensures that their wisdom, values, and unique way of life are passed on to future generations. By supporting the Aeta, we protect both their community and the diverse cultural fabric of the nation.

When we protect Aeta traditions, we are not just preserving history — we are ensuring that their children grow up proud of who they are, with their language, music, and rituals still alive and celebrated. Supporting the Aeta also strengthens the diverse cultural fabric of the nation, reminding us that true progress is achieved when no community is left behind. Cultural preservation is not charity — it is a shared responsibility and a commitment to honor the roots of our identity as Filipinos.
